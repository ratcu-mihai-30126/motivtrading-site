Preview lesson: breakout from consolidation

One of the cleanest setups in the academy starts with compression. Price coils into a tight range, liquidity builds on both sides, and the expansion begins once the market finally accepts beyond that range. The entry is not based on hope. It is based on location, confirmation, and a clear failure point.

Start with location. A good trigger in a bad location is still a bad trade. The setup becomes valid when price is compressed around a meaningful level.
Wait for confirmation. A real break shows acceptance and follow-through, not just a quick touch through the line.
Define invalidation before the entry. The stop belongs beneath the consolidation base or beneath the exact failure point that cancels acceptance above the level.
Map the target before the trade is live. The best targets are the next liquidity pool, prior swing high, or a measured expansion objective.

Execution principle

Inside the academy, execution is taught as a repeatable framework instead of an emotional reaction. Management decisions are made before the trade starts, not while price is moving.

Build the trade firstKnow the entry logic, stop location, target structure, and whether you take partials before the position is live.
Manage behavior, not feelingsOnce the trade is open, judge it by structure, reaction speed, and whether price behaves as expected.
Review execution after the sessionA strong review asks whether the plan was followed, not only whether the trade won.
