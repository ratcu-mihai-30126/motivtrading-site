Chapter 1

Trend, range, and transition

The market spends most of its time doing one of three things: trending, ranging, or transitioning from one condition to another. Your job is to identify which environment you are in before you place any trade. Trends reward continuation logic, ranges reward patience and edges, and transitions require extra caution because information is incomplete.

Chapter 2

How to mark the right swings

Not every candle high or low matters. Focus on meaningful swing points that produced displacement, rejection, or a strong reaction. Those points reveal where one side defended control. When the market returns there, you can judge whether that control is still present or has begun to fail.

Chapter 3

Liquidity sweep versus break of structure

A sweep takes liquidity and then reclaims the level. A real break of structure shows acceptance beyond the level and follow-through. Confusing the two leads traders to fade strength too early or chase reversals that never confirm. Wait for the market to prove that structure has changed, not just touch a line on the chart.

Chapter 4

Turn structure into a trade plan

Once structure is clear, the plan gets simpler: identify the side in control, define the area that matters, and decide what confirmation would justify risk. Structure tells you where the market is likely to react, but your plan should also define where that idea fails and what not to do if price sits in the middle of nowhere.

Key concepts

What matters most in this module

Protected highs and lowsThese are the swing points that matter because they defended trend structure or triggered displacement.
Control versus failureA trend remains valid until the structure that supports it clearly breaks.

How to review this setup like a trader

Structure • Trend transition example

Downtrend Breakout and Acceptance

This chart fits the market structure module because students can clearly study where sellers controlled price, where structure shifted, and how acceptance after the break matters more than a simple touch through the level.

Mark the protected swing, the level that broke, and the zone where price proved acceptance rather than a simple sweep.

Explanation

Structure is not just highs and lows on a chart. It is the sequence of defended levels that tells you who is in control. The most important lesson here is identifying when that sequence actually changes.

Entry

Enter after the break shows acceptance beyond structure, not on the very first poke through the level.

Stop

Keep the stop beyond the structural point that should hold if the break is genuine and not just a liquidity grab.

Targets

Use the next clean liquidity objective or prior reaction zone created by the previous trend leg.
