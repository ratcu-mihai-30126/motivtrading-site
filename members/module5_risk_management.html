Chapter 1

Position sizing keeps the game fair

Position sizing converts your stop distance into a controlled amount of money at risk. Without it, every trade carries a different emotional load and your results become impossible to interpret. A trader who risks the same fraction of capital on each setup can evaluate the system clearly and survive losing streaks without panic.

Chapter 2

Daily loss limits and stop conditions

Every professional needs a point where the day is over. A daily loss limit protects you from emotional escalation, revenge trading, and poor decisions during low-quality conditions. Good stop conditions can also be non-monetary: two rule-breaking trades, a loss of focus, or a session that no longer matches your edge.

Chapter 3

How to handle winning and losing streaks

Losing streaks tempt traders to force recoveries. Winning streaks tempt them to become careless and oversized. Both are dangerous. The right response is to anchor yourself to fixed risk and objective review. If the process is intact, keep size stable. If the process is breaking down, reduce risk and simplify until consistency returns.

Chapter 4

Think like a business, not a gambler

Trading improves when you treat it like a business with measurable inputs. Track average risk, win rate, reward-to-risk, maximum drawdown, and whether you followed the plan. Those numbers tell you whether the system has edge and whether you are executing it well. The business mindset removes drama and adds feedback.

Two-Target Breakout Management

This chart belongs in risk management because it teaches what to do after entry: how to scale partials, how to protect capital after the first objective is reached, and how to avoid turning a good trade into an emotional decision.

Use the first target to reduce risk, then manage the second target according to structure instead of greed.

Explanation

Risk management is not only about where the stop goes. It is also about how the trade is managed as it moves in your favor. Students should learn how partial profits reduce emotional pressure and protect expectancy.

Entry

Enter at the breakout confirmation according to the playbook, with position size already calculated from the stop distance.

Stop

Place the stop below the structure that invalidates the breakout, then consider moving to breakeven only after the first objective is reached.

Targets

Take partial profits at target one, then let the remainder work toward the second objective only while structure continues to support the trade.
